Viking Sword

Viking swords were double-edged, often about 90 cm long, and designed for cutting and slashing. Crafted from high-quality iron or steel, they featured intricate decorations on the hilt and pommel. Some swords bore the “Ulfberht” signature, indicating superior craftsmanship, making them prized in Viking society.
